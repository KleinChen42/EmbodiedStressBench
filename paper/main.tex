\title{EmbodiedStressBench: Reproducible Simulation Stress Tests for Language-to-Target Generation in Robotic Manipulation}
\author{Zhuo Chen}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Language-conditioned manipulation systems often hide target-generation failures behind aggregate task success. We present EmbodiedStressBench, a reproducible simulation study for stress-testing the language-query-to-3D-target stage of robotic manipulation. The study separates semantic target selection, RGB-D target lifting, depth and occlusion corruption, and execution-tolerance perturbations through parameterized stressors, per-episode artifacts, oracle-gap analysis, and a failure taxonomy. Across large-scale ManiSkill diagnostic sweeps, target sources show a precision--robustness tradeoff: box-center depth is more precise under strict distance thresholds, while crop-median depth is stronger at the default and relaxed thresholds. A learned-detector bridge shows that open-vocabulary detectors can be evaluated through the same protocol, and a YCB/clutter held-out extension exposes detector-query transfer failure through no-detection outcomes. A scripted closed-loop smoke test did not pass the oracle execution gate, so execution calibration is treated as an unresolved limitation rather than a positive claim. EmbodiedStressBench is therefore positioned as a detector-agnostic diagnostic substrate for reproducible target-generation failure analysis, not as a new closed-loop policy benchmark or real-robot system.
\end{abstract}
