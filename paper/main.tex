\begin{abstract}
Language-conditioned embodied manipulation pipelines are often evaluated under clean simulation settings, where aggregate success rates can hide fragility in semantic grounding, RGB-D target generation, geometric consistency, and execution. We propose EmbodiedStressBench, a simulation-based diagnostic benchmark for stress-testing such pipelines under semantic, visual, geometric, and execution perturbations. The benchmark records structured per-run metrics, failure modes, and oracle-gap measurements between privileged targets and predicted executable 3D targets. This draft intentionally avoids unsupported numerical claims; all quantitative results should be inserted only after being generated by the released scripts.
\end{abstract}
