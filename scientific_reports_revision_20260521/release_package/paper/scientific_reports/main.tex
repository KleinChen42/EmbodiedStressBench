\title{EmbodiedStressBench: A Reproducible Simulation Diagnostic for Query-Conditioned 3D Target Localization in Robotic Manipulation}
\author{Zhuo Chen\\EmbodiedStressBench Project\\Correspondence: author email to be finalized before submission}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
Language-conditioned manipulation systems often hide target-generation failures behind aggregate task success. We present EmbodiedStressBench, a reproducible simulation study for stress-testing the language-query-to-3D-target stage of robotic manipulation. The study separates target selection, RGB-D target lifting, detection-list semantic ambiguity, depth and occlusion corruption, and target-displacement tolerance through parameterized stressors, per-episode artifacts, oracle-gap analysis, and a failure taxonomy. Across large-scale ManiSkill diagnostic sweeps, target sources show a precision--robustness tradeoff: box-center depth is more precise under strict distance thresholds, while crop-median depth is stronger at the default and relaxed thresholds. A learned-detector bridge shows that open-vocabulary detectors can be evaluated through the same protocol, and YCB/clutter query checks expose detector and adapter-label limitations without relying on positive closed-loop task-success claims. A scripted closed-loop smoke test did not pass the oracle execution gate, so execution calibration is treated as an unresolved limitation rather than a positive claim. EmbodiedStressBench is therefore positioned as a detector-agnostic diagnostic substrate for reproducible query-conditioned 3D target-localization failure analysis, not as a new closed-loop policy benchmark or real-robot system.
\end{abstract}
